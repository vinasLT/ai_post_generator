## Selection Criteria

### EXCLUDE lots with these documents/titles:
- Certificate of Destruction
- Bill of Sale
- Parts Only
- Non-Repairable
- Junk
- Irreparable/Irrepairable (account for spelling variations)

### ACCEPTABLE Primary Damage Types:
- MINOR DENT/SCRATCHES
- NORMAL WEAR
- HAIL
- PARTIAL REPAIR
- REAR END
- SIDE
- TOP/ROOF
- INTERIOR

### UNACCEPTABLE Primary Damage Types (EXCLUDE):
- BIOHAZARD/CHEMICAL
- BURN
- ENGINE BURN
- ENGINE DAMAGE
- FRAME DAMAGE
- FRONT END
- FRONT & REAR
- ELECTRICAL
- MECHANICAL
- MISSING/ALTERED VIN
- ROLLOVER
- STORM DAMAGE
- STRIPPED
- SUSPENSION
- THEFT
- Transmission Damage
- VANDALIZED
- UNDERCARRIAGE
- WATER/FLOOD
- REPOSSESSION
- REPLACED VIN
- REJECTED REPAIR

### Additional Priority Factors:
1. **Keys availability** - Strongly prefer lots with Keys: Yes
2. **Vehicle status** - "Run & Drive" is preferable to "Stationary" or "Unknown"
3. **Odometer status** - "Actual" mileage is preferable to "Not Actual" or "TMU"
4. **Document type** - Clear Title or Salvage Title are acceptable (unless from an excluded list above)

Only include lots that pass the quality criteria.

Focus on selecting lots with the best potential for resale value and customer interest, prioritizing those with minimal damage and good documentation.

IMAGES DESCRIPTION PROCESSING:
CRITICAL DAMAGE to look for:
- Structural damage (frame deformation, pillars, door sills)
- Engine bay and motor compartment damage
- Severe body damage (deep dents, metal tears)
- Suspension and drivetrain damage
- Flood damage signs (corrosion, dirt, silt in interior/trunk)
- Fire damage signs (burned components, soot)
- Missing critical parts (engine, transmission, airbags)
- Severe interior damage and safety system damage

Focus only on important details, ignore minor scratches and cosmetic defects, do not look for perfect condition.
